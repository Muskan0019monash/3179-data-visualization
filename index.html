Global Migration to Australia Dashboard

Interactive Analysis of Global Migration Patterns and Trends

About This Dashboard

Welcome to the Global Migration to Australia Dashboard, an interactive visualization tool that explores migration patterns and trends to Australia from around the world. This dashboard provides insights into the diverse origins of migrants, their settlement patterns across Australian states, and how migration has evolved over time.

Australia has long been a destination for migrants seeking new opportunities, with over 30% of its population born overseas. The data reveals fascinating trends, including the shift from traditional European migration sources to increasing numbers from Asian countries, reflecting Australia's growing economic and cultural ties with the Asia-Pacific region.

Explore the interactive charts below to discover how migration policies, economic conditions, and global events have shaped Australia's demographic landscape over the past decade.

Global Migration Flow

Insight: This map visualizes the global distribution of migrants to Australia. The United Kingdom leads with over 1 million migrants, followed by China and India. The map highlights Australia's position as a "melting pot" of cultures from around the world.

State Settlement Patterns

Insight: New South Wales and Victoria are the primary gateways for migrants, attracting over 60% of newcomers. These states offer the "golden ticket" with established migrant communities and diverse economic opportunities.

Regional Migration Sources

Insight: Asia dominates as the primary source region, representing a "changing of the guard" from traditional European migration. This shift reflects Australia's geographic position and growing economic ties with Asian nations.

Top Source Countries

Insight: The United Kingdom remains the "old faithful" with the highest migrant numbers, while China and India represent the "new frontier" of migration. This demonstrates Australia's ability to maintain traditional ties while embracing new relationships.

Migration Trends Over Time

Insight: This line chart shows migration trends over time, highlighting the significant impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020-21. The recovery since then demonstrates Australia's continued appeal as a migration destination.

Visa & Citizenship Groups

Insight: Temporary visa holders have become the dominant group in recent years, reflecting Australia's shift toward temporary migration pathways. This "flexible workforce" approach allows Australia to respond to changing economic needs.
